Opponent has you in half guard defence

  • TIP: Immediately stop his underarm + try to flatten opponent.

  • Whatever leg he's trapped, your heap goes on his hip that side. i.e. if he has your right leg,
    your face goes on his right side. Left arm goes under his leg, grab your own right wrist. Go down,
    keep hip low, kick right leg back to escape. right arm then grabs both of his legs - go into side control.

  • He half guards your right leg. Left arm round his head. Your head on his left side. Arm goes around his head
    and control tight with your elbow, keep his upper body down. Change your hip, knee out first.
    Other leg go up, control his hip. If can't get leg out, other leg keep one of his legs down.

  • He half guards your right leg. Left arm around his head deep. Left leg kick back so he cant grab knee.
    With your right hand, get his lapel out of his belt and pass it to your left hand, pressure on his head
    Move left knee down, get right knee out, then use ballerina foot to get foot out.
    Or when have knee out, get underarm and put right knee on floor and go into mount.

  • He has your right leg in half guard. You have both knees on the floor. Left arm around his head, right hand
    have his underarm under his left arm. Move your left knee under his legs.
    Option 1: Push his knee to get your right knee out. Get right hand underarm, put right knee on floor.
    Get both underarms, use one leg to push his leg and escape your leg and into mount.
    Option 2: Push his knee to get your right knee out. Right underarm. Put right knee on his belly and left leg out.
    Move knee in semicircle across floor (Jackson Sousa) then into side control.

  • He half guards your right leg. Left arm round his head. Switch hip to the side to free the knee. Pull gi
    out of his belt with your right hand. He tries to underarm your right arm for a sweep.
    Option 1: Your right arm go under his underarm. Left hand passes the gi to your right hand under his head
    Grab end of the gi, not deep. Pull the gi + pressure with your shoulder.
    Option 2 (Better): If gi too small, go over his underarm to grab gi. Posture up to break his underarm grip, then
    pass that arm across his face.
