Defend Bad Positions

  • When you're in omoplata, roll and take side control.

  • When escaping the mount and his knees on your armpits. Push his hips and move back. Create space first
    Otherwise, too easy for them to escape your leg trap.

  • You on your back, they're standing. Grab cross lapel with your right arm, and left arm back for support.
    They try to step around: you follow. When opponent passes both legs to your right -> pull hard with your right arm
    to take them down into turtle. Right hand goes on floor, left arm goes round to take back cross lapel. Left knee up. right knee down.

  • Both have right-handed Judo grip. Opponent pulls guard. When pull guard, foot on side that holds sleeve goes on opponents hip.
    Your hand on their sleeve, let go and use to push their leg to the side and take side control. Can also use your foot
    to sweep their leg when they have one leg on your hip.

  • Escape from side control half guard. You have their right leg in half guard but they've managed to flatten you out.
    First get left hand on right side of their neck. Can pull down on their left shoulder with your right hand to help you do this
    Be careful not to go high or will put you in kata gatame. Create space with your left hand and straighten out your neck
    Want to put right elbow inside of their left knee. If they're very tight, shrimp to the left to create space and put
    your elbow in. Then can shrimp out a bit more and then bring hips back to the middle, at the same time pushing their
    knee away to create a lot of space. Keep their knee away. Shrimp to the left again, keeping their knee away
    Open half guard. Left leg hooks their left leg to stop them passing. Bring right knee through to the other side as move
    hips back. Retake guard. Can stretch out left leg to help.
